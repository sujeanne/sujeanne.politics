Election Guide for Progressives

November 8 2022 (Ballots will be mailed October 21)

SAVE THE DATE: Saturday, Oct 1 3pm-5pm. Meet the Candidates at 5354 Lehigh Lane in Sedalia. Drinks and munchies!

Kari Solberg, Douglas County Commisioner
Ethical leadership that represents the people, collaborates well, and gets things done. Cares for the environment, safe communities, supports those less fotunate. Campaign: https://www.kariforcommissioner.com
Mike Phibbs, Douglas County Sheriff
An unaffiliated candidate, Phibbs has 30 years of experience in law enforcement. Public safety and public trust, not party politics. Campaign: https://www.phibbs4sheriff.com.
Karen Lindberg Jefferson, Douglas County Clerk
Karen is a Denver native and entreprenuerial businesssperson. Karen hasn't worked in government before so does she does not have old habits or political favors to repay. Karen is thoughtful, transparent and trustworthy. She knows that democracy is at stake in this election and vows to follow the law, speak the truth, and protect your vote. Your vote is your voice. Campaign: https://www.YayKLJ4Doug.co
Robert "Jack" Barrington, University of Colorado Regent District 4
Barrington resides in Las Animas. He earned his bachelor's degree after serving in the U.S. Navy for 4 years. As a science teacher, he integrated new technology into the school's curriculum. He currently manages an auto parts store in Las Animas where he served as chair of the Bent County Democratic Party. He believes that a regent should have empathy and consideration for all constituents' issues and needs, and that the position should not be political. (A regent serves on an independent governing body that oversees a state's public colleges and universities.) Campaign: https://www.facebook.com/BarringtonforCD4
Phil Weiser, Colorado Attorney General
Weiser is running for second term as Colorado Attorney General He has provided strong leadership in protecting state land, air and water. He prides himself in serving all people of the state regardless of position or party. His professional background includes experience both in academics and government. He was dean of the University of Colorado Law School, served as a senior advisor in the Obama Administration and on the President's transition team. He served as a law clerk for both Supreme Court Justices Ruth Bader Ginsburg and Byron R White. Campaign: https://www.philforcolorado.com
Jenna Griswold, Colorado Secretary of State
No state political contest is more important than the re-election of Griswold to a second term as Secretary of State. She is a stalwart defender of free and fair elections for all voters of all parties throughout the state. She has expanded access to voting and provided agressive leadership in protecting cyber security against foreign election interference. She is an attorney who practiced in Louisville, Colorado, and once served as the Director of the Governor's D.C. office. Campaign: https://www.jenaforcolorado.com
Dave Young, Colorado State Treasurer
Young is seeking a second term as State Treasurer. He is a strong advocate of economic justice, including college savings accounts, small business funding and the Colorado Secure Savings Program, finding owners of unclaimed property, and support of a solvent public employee retirement system. After a career as a science and math teacher in Greeley, Young was appointed to the state house in 2011 and elected for two more terms. He served on the powerful state Joint Budget Committee and the House Appropriations Committee. Campaign: https://www.daveyoungforcolorado.com
Kathy Plomer, Colorado State Board of Education At Large
Plomer served for eight years on the Adams School District Board of Education, including six years as the board president. While serving, the district passed a bond and a mill levy override, improved graduation rates, and added career and technical education opportunities for students. Adams 12 was named a performance rated district. She believes that Colorado can become a national leader in education through collaboration among all stakeholders. Campaign: https://www.plomerforcolorado.com
Eric Brody, Colorado House District 39 (includes: Roxborough Park, Sedalia, Franktown, Perry Park, Larkspur, Castle Pines.)
Brody has been passionately engaged in politics and public affairs his entire adult life, beginning with his leadership of the Independent Party of the Yale Political Union. His pursuit of continued professionally in various roles, including on the staff of a U.S. Representative, as a project assistant for the National Democratic Institute for international Affairs (NDI), as a researcher for Media Matters for America, and as an issue advocate for Landslide Political. His volunteer contributions include his active involvement in the Douglas County Democrats, where he is the organizer for precinct 261, as president of the board of directors of the Transcendental Politics Foundation, and as member of the steering committee for the Southern Front Range Alliance chapter of Braver Angels. Priority challenges: wildfires, depletion of water resources, behavioral health, education. Campaign: https://www.brodyforcoloradohd39.com. Contact: Eric@BRODYforColoradoHD39.com
Jeff Ravage, Colorado Senate District 4 (Includes: Castle Pines, Castle Rock, Sedalia, Larkspur, Parker, Perry Park, The Pinery, and the southern tip of Aurora.)
Jeff supports a living wage, unions, Social Security, civil rights, Medicare for All, lower drug prices. Healthcare is a human right. Campaign: https://www.ravage4.com
Jared Polis, Colorado Governor
Passed bipartisan legislation that will send every taxpayer who lives in Colorado a $750 rebate in 2022 to help folks with rising costs. Starting 2023, preschool in Colorado will be free for every four-year-old, saving parents with preschoolers an average of $4,300. Put a cap on the price of insulin to no more than $100 a month. Working to save families money -- including making it free to start a business and ending the state tax on seniors' social security benefits. Campaign: https://polisforcolorado.com
Dianne Primavera, Lt. Governor
Primavera is a strong supporter of affordable health care. Before entering politics, she worked for the Rocky Mountain Brain Injury Center, and served as Colorado CEO of the Susan G. Komen Foundation. Background: https://www.colorado.gov/governor/lt-governor-primavera
Michael Bennet, U.S. Senate
Bennet has held his Senate seat since 2009. He has championed issues such as fighting inflation, opposing tax breaks for the very wealthy, and lowering health care costs; creating good paying jobs. Working to ban members of Congress from becoming lobbyists. He rejects corporate PAC donations and federal lobbyist money. He wants to protect public lands, combat climate change, to prevent wildfires and drought. Campaign: https://michaelbennet.com
Ike McCorkle, U.S. Congressional District 4 (Includes: eastern plains, Greeley, Longmont, Castle Rock, Parker, Perry Park, Larkspur, Louviers, Roxborough Park, and Sedalia.)
McCorkle is a Marine Corp verteran, purple heart recipient, father, welder, CU alum, and a small business owner. He supports economic security, jobs, pensions, civil rights, healthcare for women, living wage, unions, Social Security, Medicare for all, lower drug costs, and rural reinvestment. Campaign: https://ike4co.com. Contact: campaign@ike4co.com
